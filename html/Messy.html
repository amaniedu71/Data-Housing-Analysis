Analysing Housing Conditions in Ames, Iowa¶

Motivation¶

Given that housing is perhaps the single most expenditure item that a household in the United States is paying for, housing affordability is a very relevant and pertinent issue. In fact according to a paper by Quigley and Raphael, 2004, most poor and near-poor families often devote half their income to housing. There is a large demand for the supply of affordable housing to households of all income levels but due to factors ranging from income inequality to racism or political injustice, such as a supply is often not available in many US cities and states. In this data analysis, I wish to explore how socio-demographic or economic factors affect or corellate with monthly housing expenditure in New York city. The reason New York was chosen is two-fold. Firstly, according to Economic Policy Institute New York is the state with the by the most Income Inequality as of 2015. Secondly, New York has ≈ 100, 000 observations which are enough for sampling methedoogy and I am confident represent households from every income class. Although I do not have an extensive background in economics or public policy, by conducting a minor literature review, I have chosen to consider these socio-demographic and economic characteristics:

  • household montly income
  • household poverty status
  • household head race
  • household ethnicity
  • household head gender
  • household family status
  • household head working status
  • household number of income providers
  • household geographical location. In attempts of segmenting or stratifying housing costs or finding a corellation between any number of these characteristics housing costs.

A literature lead me to a paper Is there a corellation between Socio-demographic characteristics and race, household head gender, household family status, household head working status, household number of income providers and household geographical location.

My hypothesis is that a dire state of Housing price and how it is affected by household's income Is there segemantation based on income?
Housing price affectd by other necessary amenities of a house: toilet, electricity (things you can't get by without) Do the above things only belong to the rich?

Questions¶

I further explore affordability among populations stratified by socio-economic and demographic characteristics. Examples of questions I wish to explore:

  • Does family income corellate to housing cost and value of property if house is owned?
  • Can the mean housing cost as a percentage of household income across different aforementioned socio-demographic factors be quantitatively expressed?
  • Is the tenure of the house (morgaged, rented, fully owned) affected by aforementioned factors? For example, I do not expect that households in the lowest percentile of income own their own houses.
  • Cars are an asset that may be an indicator of a household's income class. Do number of cars corellate with housing costs?

Data¶

The data used was from the 2013 version of the American Community Survey which surveys over 3.5 million households about different socio-demographic characteristics across the United States. The data is divided into two categories, Housing records and Person records which are matched by a unique serial number. The former gives data about of locational and dwelling unit characteristic and the latter about socio-economic and demographic characteristics of those living in the households.   plt.hist(x, bins = bins)
